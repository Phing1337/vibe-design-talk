## Key Themes from the Original Talk

### 1. The Dynamic Medium (Bret Victor)
> "A dynamic medium doesn't just present ideas, it responds to them. It adapts, evolves, and shapes itself around how we think, feel, and create."

- The computer isn't just a tool — it's a medium for thought and expression
- When tools adapt to us (instead of the other way around), they open new ways of thinking
- Video games are the purest example: rich, responsive systems with continuous dialogue between creator, user, and system

**→ Maps to new Beat 2 (The Reframe):** This is the philosophical foundation. Open with this.

### 2. Our Tools Don't Fit
> "Even our most creative tools — Illustrator, Excel, code editors — don't feel human. They're rigid. They force you to think in machine terms, not human ones."

- Current tools are built around abstraction, not expression
- Humans shouldn't have to learn the computer's language — the computer should learn ours
- The evolution: Coding → Direct manipulation, Procedures → Goals and constraints, Text input → Multimodal

**→ Maps to new Beat 2 (The Reframe):** Sets up WHY vibe design matters — we're finally getting tools that work the way humans think.

### 3. General Purpose Technology
- AI is a GPT like writing, the printing press, electricity, computers
- These technologies apply lateral growth — they change everything, not just one domain
- AI model growth rate: 2x every 2 months (vs Moore's law 2x every 2 years)

**→ Maps to new Beat 2 (The Reframe):** Context for why this matters NOW.

### 4. The Collaboration Matrix
| Role | Shift |
|---|---|
| Non-designers | Bad design → Good design |
| Designers | Good design → Great design |
| Non-developers | Static things → Dynamic things |
| Developers | Good code → Great code |

**→ Maps to new Beat 2/3:** This is a powerful visual. Reuse it.

### 5. AI as Translation
> "LLMs are fantastic translation tools — more than language, they can translate ideas into code, images, music. These are secondary faculties we can use when solving problems."

- What if your brain could convey your idea better?
- What if other brains could communicate their contributions in a way you understood?
- The outcome could be greater than the sum of its parts

**→ Maps to new Beat 3 (Vocabulary) and Beat 4 (Workflow):** This is the core argument for WHY vocabulary matters — AI translates your intent, but the quality of translation depends on the clarity of your input.

### 6. Key Prediction (from 2024)
> "AI will be better than you at creating familiar user interactions with beautiful UIs. However, novel solutions may still require human orchestration, architecture design, and decision-making when directions are unclear."

**→ Maps to new "What This IS / What This ISN'T":** Still true. Reinforce it.

### 7. Local/Community-Driven Software
- Purpose-built tools, truly personal and private
- "The right tool for the right job"
- Commercial software is a carrier when most people need a dinghy

**→ Maps to new Beat 5 (Takeaway):** The vision — what's possible when everyone can vibe design.

### 8. Tactics for Enablement
- Free their data (export/import)
- Standardization and protocols
- Consider how AI agents would use your product
- Define "human in the loop"

**→ Less relevant to new talk format, but could inform the takeaway/resources section**

## Speaker Notes Worth Preserving

**Slide 1 (personal motivation):**
> "I started this in August 2024. My son had just turned one. And I was hit with this wave of uncertainty — what do I tell him? What do I teach him? The advice I grew up with didn't seem to hold anymore."

*This personal angle could be a powerful callback in the new talk. The 2024 talk was about uncertainty. The 2026 talk is about what you've learned since.*

**Slide 11 (the bridge):**
> "Bret's ideas are amazing, but he possesses a skill that others don't have yet — command over the medium. Communicating intent to a computer is its own line of science, but new tools and technology are enabling a new set of skills."

*This is literally the thesis of the new talk. Bret Victor had command over the medium. Now anyone can — IF they have the vocabulary.*
