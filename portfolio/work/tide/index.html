Tide

A system-tray companion that keeps utilities one keystroke away.

links
figma Figma file ↗ proto Prototype ↗

Tide is a low-chrome command surface that lives at the edge of the screen. The goal: cover 80% of small utility tasks without ever taking focus away from the thing in front of you.

The problem

Power users build up a long tail of small actions — convert this, jump to that, paste from history — and the cost of leaving the current task to perform any one of them is what makes the day feel choppy.

What we explored

A keyboard-first card stack that opens beside the cursor, a quiet result list, and a deliberate refusal to grow into a full launcher. Every interaction had to either return you to your task or finish in a single keystroke.

Where it landed

A small set of card primitives that compose into most of the use cases tested in research. Hand-off ready, with motion specs and a couple of edge-case rules I had to learn the hard way.
